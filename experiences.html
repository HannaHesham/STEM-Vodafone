Hanna Hesham

As this is my first time joining a program, I should say: this is a never miss experience! Vodafone's STEM program exposed me to so much more than I could have imagined in such a limited time, i experienced new things, and learned so much on different levels.


  • Technical level:

    • Basically I've seen another side of the technology! I was introduced to what I thought never existed. Our coaches were able to explain the concept of coding to me, from the basics , to the complex elements that we don't even have to use. I learned 3 new technical languages ( HTML, CSS, and JAVASCRIPT) I found meaning and excitement in them. We were taken on the journey of website creation, the process of using HTML to create the content, the CSS to design and give life to the website, and finally JAVASCRIPT to animate! By the end of this program, I am able to look at a page full of codes and tags yet understand what they mean, and already have an idea on what the website might look like.



  • General skills:
    • I was able to develop and improve different general skills such as:
    1. confidence
    2. team work
    3. Learning

    The whole program is based on the interaction with those around us (our coaches, other participants, organisers etc..). To start with, my self-confidence way improved considering the scale of the organisation, communicating with new faces, including adults.

    Since the beginning of the program we worked as groups to deliver our ideas, some of the games we played were totally based on team work, on understanding each other sometimes even without speaking. But i developed more by working with my partner for the creation of the website, we learned to accept each other’s ideas to survive, to work fast together in such limited time and to complete each other’s work for the best results.

    The pressure was learning so much, in such limited time. But as hard as this sounds, I was really able to improve my learning skills as we kept practicing everything new we learned. We created or own HTMLs, and linked them to CSSs and practiced so much more a long the way. So as I was practicing and taking notes unconsciously I was developing my learning skills, and I understood the materials in 5 days.
